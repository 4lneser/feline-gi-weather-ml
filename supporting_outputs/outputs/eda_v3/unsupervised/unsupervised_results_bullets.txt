PC1–PC3 interpretation (top loading patterns)

  - PC1 is a precipitation burden axis (precipitation_hours/sum means and peaks positive) opposed to pressure (surface_pressure negative).
  - PC2 is a snowfall axis (snowfall windows positive) opposed to warmer/daylight/wind-direction conditions (apparent_temperature/daylight/wind-direction negative).
  - PC3 is wind-gust intensity (wind_gusts positive) opposed to precipitation-peak/wind-direction peak patterns (negative).
- clinical_only:
  - PC1 is dominated by coordinated missingness indicators for core labs (electrolytes/creatinine/protein missing flags positive), not a pure biology axis.
  - PC2 contrasts inflammatory/azotemia markers (leukocyte, neutrophil, BUN, albumin positive) versus chloride/sodium (negative).
  - PC3 reflects protein/hematocrit/body-mass status (albumin, hematocrit, total protein, body weight positive) opposed to BUN/hospitalization burden (negative).
- combined:
  - PC1 is again largely a missingness-pattern axis from clinical missing indicators.
  - PC2 recreates the environmental precipitation-versus-pressure axis.
  - PC3 recreates snowfall versus warmer/daylight/wind-direction contrast.
